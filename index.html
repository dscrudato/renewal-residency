What is the Renewal Residency?

Where kingdom mission
meets the
marketplace.

The Renewal Residency is a program of Summit Church in Raleigh-Durham, NC. We're combining the best of talent development and global mobility from the corporate world with best-in-class ministry and missions leadership development — forming a 2-year residency unlike anything else.

Graduates leave equipped to live out their calling in the marketplace and help bring about kingdom restoration in the world through their daily work.

If you believe God has more for your work than it just being a means to an end — this is for you.

Is This For You?

Built for those who refuse to settle.

This IS for you if...

You believe your work is part of God's design for the world.

  • You don't want to separate faith from your career
  • You're hungry to grow and be developed — spiritually, personally, professionally
  • You want clarity, direction, and mentorship
  • You want to understand how your job plays a role in God's plan to reach the world

You see work as simply a paycheck and nothing more.

Program Structure

What does a
typical month look like?

1

Monthly Cohort Forum

The whole cohort gathers together. You'll hear from different industry speakers and discuss curated content from books, articles, and podcasts explored in your own time.

2

Peer Small Groups

Gather with 1–2 other residents twice a month outside of the forum to discuss content and wrestle with how it applies to your specific work and context.

3

Leadership Development Guide

Meet monthly with a guide matched to your industry. Together you'll build a personalized career development plan over the two years — someone investing directly in where you're going.

Common Questions

Still have
questions?

We want to make sure this is the right fit before you apply. If you don't see your question here, reach out to Julie directly at jscrudato@summitchurch.com.

Is this only for people at Summit Church?

Currently, yes — Renewal Residency is a program of Summit Church and membership is required to participate. We're deeply committed to this being rooted in a local church community, not just a standalone program. That said, we hope to expand access in the future, so if you're not yet a member but are interested, reach out and let's talk.

How much does it cost?

The total program fee is $5,000 — a one-time investment for the full two years. This covers travel expenses for vision trips, forum speakers, curated materials and content, experiential programming, and the various costs that make the residency what it is. If cost is a barrier, reach out — we want to have that conversation.

Can I do this while working full-time?

Yes — the program is designed for working professionals. The monthly commitment is 5–10 hours and all core activities are scheduled with full-time work in mind. The international and domestic trips will require some PTO.

What industries is this designed for?

All of them. We've had residents from business, healthcare, education, tech, law, finance, entrepreneurship, and more. Your guide will be matched from your specific industry, so the mentorship is always directly relevant to your work.

When does the next cohort begin?

The next cohort begins in Fall 2026. Applications close May 31, 2026. After submitting your application, you'll be invited to an interest meeting and a brief interview before a final decision is made.

How many people are in a cohort?

Cohorts are kept intentionally small — around 15–20 people — to maintain a high-quality experience and genuine community. We limit enrollment to ensure every resident gets meaningful attention from their guide and real relationships within the cohort.

What happens after the two years?

You become part of the Renewal alumni network — a growing global community of professionals who share your conviction that work and faith belong together. Through the international and domestic trips, you'll have built relationships with leaders and church planters around the world. That network doesn't end at graduation; it's one of the most lasting things you take with you.
